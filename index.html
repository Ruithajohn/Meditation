7 Types of Meditations

Mindfulness Meditation

Involves paying attention to your thoughts and feelings without judgment, often focusing on the breath. It's widely practiced in Western cultures and rooted in Buddhist traditions.

Loving-Kindness Meditation (Metta)

Focuses on cultivating an attitude of love and compassion toward yourself and others. It involves silently repeating positive phrases or affirmations.

Body Scan Meditation

This technique involves mentally scanning your body for tension or discomfort, aiming to release stress and deepen awareness of physical sensations.

Zen Meditation (Zazen)

A seated form of meditation from Zen Buddhism, where the practitioner focuses on breathing or simply "being" in the present moment, often with the guidance of a teacher.

Transcendental Meditation

A specific technique where practitioners silently repeat a mantra to achieve a deep state of relaxation and heightened awareness. This form was made popular in the West by Maharishi Mahesh Yogi.

Vipassana Meditation

A traditional Buddhist practice focused on gaining insight into the true nature of reality. It involves observing thoughts and feelings as they arise, fostering mindfulness and understanding.
